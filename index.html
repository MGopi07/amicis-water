FAQ’s

Common Questions Answered

Chlorine dioxide, despite its name, differs chemically from chlorine. Comprising one chlorine and two oxygen atoms, it forms a molecular free radical with a magnetic-like attraction that targets and oxidizes harmful bacteria and mold.

Chlorine Dioxide is literally used in all the industries for the water treatment and disinfection. Be it the human drinking water, food and beverage processing, swimming pool, hotels and used as a bleaching agent in the paper industry and oil refineries.

Every manufacturer has different formulations and ways for manufacturing Chlorine Dioxide. Staroxide is uniquely formulated, it is Non-Irritating to the skin and is Non-Corrosive to the pipelines and equipments, saving a ton of money for the customers.

The DBP’s of Chlorine Dioxide are Sodium Chlorite, Sodium Chlorate and ultimately Sodium Chloride, Nacl, the normal salt. The concentration of the DBP’s totally depends on the concentration of Chlorine Dioxide used and in what volume of water.

Not at all! Our Staroxide Chlorine Dioxide is pH independent, i.e. it is unaffected by the pH of the water and does not lose its effectiveness even when the pH is above 10 or 11.
